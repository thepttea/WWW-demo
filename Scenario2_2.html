Detailed PR Strategy Description

Round 1: Initial Response & Transparency

Immediately issue a public statement acknowledging the product controversy. Use all major social media channels and a dedicated page on the corporate website. The message must be empathetic, transparent about what is known, and commit to a full investigation. Avoid defensive language. The goal is to control the narrative early and show the company is taking the issue seriously.

Round 2: Technical Fix & User Compensation

Announce a timeline for a software update that will remove or alter the controversial feature. Offer a form of compensation to affected users, such as a service credit or a free month of subscription. This action demonstrates a tangible commitment to resolving the problem and values the customer base.

Round 3: Leadership Communication & Future Prevention

The CEO should publish a detailed letter explaining the missteps and outlining new internal review processes to prevent similar issues in the future. This move aims to rebuild trust at a higher level and shows long-term commitment to ethical product development. Engage with tech journalists and influencers to amplify this message.

Round 4: Community Engagement & Feedback Loop

Create a public beta testing program for future features. Host a series of town hall meetings (virtual) with users to gather feedback and answer questions directly. This fosters a sense of community and shows that user feedback is now an integral part of the development process.
